About the Game

Luminara is a cozy 3D adventure game where the player controls a fairy guardian whose forest has been consumed by darkness. The small spirits that once lived in the forest have vanished — scattered and hiding somewhere in the corrupted environment. The fairy must wander the forest, find each spirit, and perform a purification ritual to restore them.

The game is designed around quiet exploration rather than combat or time pressure. Finding each spirit requires observing the environment carefully, as they blend into the darkened surroundings. Once located, the purification mechanic cleanses them, gradually returning color and light to the forest around the player.

Luminara is a solo Unity 3D project — all gameplay code, systems, and scene logic built entirely by me, working within a 3-person team where others handled art and audio.

My Contributions

Full Game Programming — Solo Solely responsible for all Unity code in the project. Every system, mechanic, and scene interaction was designed and implemented by me — the other two team members focused on art and audio.
Spirit Search & Detection System Built the system for hiding spirits within the 3D environment and detecting when the player is close enough to trigger a find event — including the visual feedback when a spirit is located.
Purification Mechanic Implemented the purification interaction — the ritual the player performs after finding a spirit that cleanses the corruption around it, tied to the game's progression and environmental visual changes.
3D Player Controller & Camera Built the fairy's 3D movement system and camera setup in Unity, tuned for the slow, exploratory pace the game is designed around.
Scene & Progression Management Managed scene state, tracking which spirits have been found and purified, and driving the environmental changes as the player restores the forest across the run.
